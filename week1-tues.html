Common Present Tense Errors for Portuguese Speakers

  • Omitting subject pronouns: Portuguese doesn't always require subject pronouns, but English does.
  • Subject-verb agreement: Remembering that "I/you/we/they" use the base form, while "he/she/it" adds "-s".
  • Present continuous vs. simple present: Using "I send now" when "I am sending now" is needed.
  • Auxiliary verbs: Using "I no like" instead of "I don't like" or "I not have" instead of "I don't have".

Rules for "Have" vs. "Has"

Subject Verb Form Example
I have I have a book.
You have You have a car.
He/She/It has He has a dog.
We have We have time.
They have They have dinner.

Remember: In the present tense, only third-person singular subjects (he, she, it) use "has". All other subjects use "have".
